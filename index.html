Llama pregnancies last almost a year or longer!

Being pregnant is hard but llamas have it a bit harder. They're usually pregnant between 342 and 368 days. Baby llamas typically weigh 20-30 pounds, and are called cria. They're on another level of cute but 20-30 pounds is terribly heavy for a baby. :O


Llamas can carry 50 pounds for 20 miles in a day!

Llamas can be useful friends! That said, you'll be hard-pressed to get an Alpaca to do the same thing, as their bone structure can't handle much more than their own body weight. Leave the packing to the heavier, stronger llamas! :D


Llamas have three stomachs! :O

Technically, we're talking "stomach chambers," but the bottom line is: Cows have four. Humans have one. Llamas have three. I'm just confused with why there are all these stomach chamber numbers. Was nature testing out the right setup? xD


Llama + Camel = Cama! :D

A female llama with a male camel can sometimes crossbreed to birth a hybrid creature called a "cama," which means bed in Spanish :P

If you put too much weight on a llama, it will refuse to move.

Llamas don't have time to be carrying all your things. You shouldn't overwork your cute friend anyway. :(
Llamas may also "spit, hiss, or even kick at their owners until their burden is lessened," says National Geographic. hehehe, kinda funny xD


Llama poop is sellable~! xD

Llama Brew is a LA-based company that sells llama poop as an all-natural fertilizer. Their tagline: "What makes Llama Brew? Llama's poo!"
